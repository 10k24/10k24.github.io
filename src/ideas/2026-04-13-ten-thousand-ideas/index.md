This is the first one.

Before AI, we might hoard notebooks or Trello boards of ideas hoping one day we might get to execute on one of them. Maybe one of those ideas could make us rich. It’s just something we did naturally as creative or tech adjacent people.

Now, in the age of genAI, we can produce endless amounts of anything: documents, images, videos, code, entire functional websites, and more. The cost of maintaining an idea has grown exponentially, not in terms of financial cost but mental bandwidth.

What was once an email or a few bullet points is now hundreds of lines of code, a very full downloads folder, several open browser tabs, and as many chat windows with various AI tools. We are so beyond information overload there is no point in describing it.

Yet, in any single moment we can only truly focus on one thing. So, what do we do when one idea can instantly carry a day’s worth of context?

Return to the [Paradox of Choice](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VO6XEQIsCoM), shout out to Barry Schwartz.

My whole life I was plagued with wanting to do 10x more than possible with the time and energy available. Until recently I felt torn between the desire to focus and being constantly inspired by another “what if?”. The dance between the two has defined my career. I always operated across disciplines. As a programmer, I thought like a designer. As a compsci student, I studied business and philosophy classes. A year after completing my MFA in Graphic Design, I can see everything clearly.

Every industry showed me a limited number of paths. During my thesis research for [Applied Designer](https://applieddesigner.com/) I found an article saying there are only four career paths of graphic designers. I was shocked my the closed-mindedness that is still so prevalent—we are creative people, why are our career paths so un-creative?

## The Ethos

It does not have to be so rigid.

10k24 is the culmination of everything I’ve ever been interested in, with a name and identity system I love. This is THE thing that can hold all 10,000 ideas despite my limited 24 hours every day.

*10k24 is built on three pillars*

1. Creativity
2. Computation
3. Collaboration

The whole reason I became a designer was a vision for the future, where I can create anything that I can think of. Over the past eight years I’ve followed this vision through fashion, graphic design, branding, typography, interactive installations, architecture, industrial design, public speaking, community building, artistic expression, and AI. This all builds on my decade of experience in mathematics, software engineering, open source, personal development, business and entrepreneurship, and philosophy. The synthesis of these diverse and complimentary areas is the unique intersection that powers everything 10k24 does.

Ultimately, 10k24 is a studio practice. We do projects that interest us with people who inspire us. If this is you, let’s talk.
